## 1. Age Categories (CRB Section 2, Rule 11 — p. S2-26)

A competitor's age is determined by how old they are as of **31 December** of the year of competition.

| Category | Eligible Ages (turns X on or before Dec 31) | Notes |
|----------|---------------------------------------------|-------|
| Youth | 15, 16, 17, 18 | Not eligible if turning 14 or 19 |
| Open | 16+ | No maximum age |
| Masters | 30+ | 5-year bands: 30-34, 35-39, … 75+ |

### Masters Relay Age Groups

| Team Size | Age Groups (sum of ages) |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| 2-person | 5-year bands from 30+ (based on youngest member) |
| 3-person | 90-109, 110-129, 130-149, 150-169, 170+ (sum of 3) |
| 4-person | 120-139, 140-169, 170-199, 200-229, 230+ (sum of 4) |

> **National federations** may define different categories. This application uses: 10-, 11-12, 13-14, 15-18, 19+/Open, Masters.

## 4. ILS Relay Rules

### 4.1 Team Composition

- A relay team consists of athletes from the **same club or national team** (CRB S3, S4).
- The number of legs is defined by the event (2, 3, or 4).
- Each athlete swims/races **only one leg** of the relay.
- One competitor completing two or more legs → **DQ (code DQ36)**.
- A competitor re-entering the water after completing their leg → **DQ (code DQ44)**.

### 4.2 Gender Rule for Mixed Events

> **CRB Section 3, Rule 18 (Pool Lifesaver Relay), p. S3-30:**  
> "For Lifesaving World Championships each team shall consist of **two males and two females**. Teams are permitted to select their own gender order."

**General mixed relay rule:**
- A mixed relay of N members requires exactly **N/2 men and N/2 women**.
- The team chooses the order of genders freely.

For M-only or F-only events, all team members must be of the specified gender.

### 4.3 Changeovers (Pool)

- In pool relays, the next swimmer may start (dive or push off) only **after** the preceding swimmer has touched the wall/edge.
- For the Manikin Relay: exchange must occur within designated **5 m changeover zones** (marked by flags/poles/cones).
- The manikin must change hands **within** the zone (judged by top of manikin's head).
- Early take-over → **DQ**.

### 4.4 Changeovers (Beach/Ocean)

- Exchanges in beach relay events are by **tagging** the next team member (CRB S4-5).
- The outgoing competitor must be positioned with feet on or on the shoreward side of the changeover line.
- Competitors must start from their designated lane/position.

### 4.5 Disqualification Codes (Relay-Specific)

| Code | Infringement | Applies To |
|------|-------------|------------|
| DQ36 | One competitor repeating two or more legs | All pool relays |
| DQ38 | Manikin changing hands outside the changeover zone | Manikin Relay, Pool Lifesaver Relay |
| DQ44 | Competitor re-entering water after completing their leg | All pool relays |
| DQ53 | Third competitor releasing wall before manikin head surfaces | Pool Lifesaver Relay |

## 5. Relay Team Composition Rules (Application-Specific)

The following rules are enforced by this application (frontend dropdown filtering + backend validation):

### 5.1 Age Group Majority Rule

The team's age group is determined by the **majority** of its members' individual registration age groups:

- **4-0**: All 4 members from same age group → valid ✓
- **3-1**: 3 from one group + 1 from another → valid ✓ (team competes in the majority group)
- **2-2**: 2 from one + 2 from another → **INVALID** ✗

**Formula**: For a relay of N members, at least ⌈N/2⌉ + 1 members must share the same age group.

### 5.2 Gender Balance (Mixed Events)

For mixed (X) events:
- 4-person relay: exactly **2 men + 2 women**
- 2-person relay: exactly **1 man + 1 woman**

### 5.3 Eligibility

An athlete is eligible for a relay team if:
1. Belongs to the same club
2. Has at least one individual event registration
3. Gender matches (or balances for mixed)
4. Not already on another team for the same event
5. Not already assigned to another position on the same team

### 5.4 Dropdown Filtering (UI Enforcement)

1. Exclude athletes assigned elsewhere on this team (intra-team)
2. Exclude athletes on another team for the same event (cross-team)
3. For mixed events: hide gender that reached quota (N/2)
4. For age groups: hide athletes that would make majority impossible

### 5.5 Team Numbering

- Teams lettered A through Z (max 26 per event/club)
- Letters are stable — deleting team B does not rename C
